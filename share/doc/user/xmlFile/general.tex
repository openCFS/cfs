\chapter{Structure of Config-File}
\label{sec:general}
%
%
The basic structure of the config-file for CFS++ in our XML
syntax is as follows:

\begin{xmllisting}
<?xml version="1.0"?>
<cfsSimulation xmlns="http://www.cfs++.org">

 ... Parameters ...

</cfsSimulation>
\end{xmllisting}

The first line gives version information for the XML parser, while the
second line is the starting tag for the main enclosing element called
\xel{cfsSimulation}.  The last line then is the closing tag for this main
element. All parameters must appear between the opening and the
closing tag.

Only a few tags are allowed in this main body. They will be documented in the
remaining sections. These top level tags have to be used in the order shown in
table \ref{tab:leavesLevelOne}.
%
%
\begin{table}
\label{tab:leavesLevelOne}
\begin{center}
\caption{Possible elements on the first leave level and their order.}
\vspace{0.5cm}
  \begin{tabular}{lll}
    \toprule
    leave name & leave type & occurence constraints \\
    \midrule
    analysis        & plane   & required\\
    geometry        & plane   & required\\
    input           & plane   & optional\\
    output          & plane   & optional\\
    materialData    & plane   & optional\\
    domain          & section & required \\
    pdeList         & section & required \\
    couplingList    & section & required for iterative or direct coupling\\
    \midrule
    \multicolumn{3}{c}{\emph{elements in the following block can appear in any
    order}}\\
    \midrule
    harmonic        & section & required for harmonic analysis \\
    transient       & section & required for transient analysis \\
    multiSequence   & section & required for multi sequence analysis \\
    bubbleDynamic   & section & optional (for simulation of bubbles) \\
    bubbleDriver    & section & optional (for simulation of bubbles) \\
    transient4Slice & section & ??? \\
    \midrule
    MpCCI           & section & optional (for coupling via MpCCI)\\
    linearSystems   & section & optional (for specifying properties of
                      linear system solvers) \\
    integRules      & section & optional (do not use)\\
    expert          & plane   & optional (for disabling expert module)\\
    devel           & plane   & optional (for developers only)\\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{center}
\end{table}
