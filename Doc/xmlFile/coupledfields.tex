\chapter{Coupled Fields}

\section{General}
According to the {\sf pdeList} we will introduce a {\sf couplingList}. Within this
list we will differ between iterative coupling (e.g. {\sf iterElecMech}) and direct
coupling (e.g. {\sf directPiezo}). For iterative coupling the method can be {\sf RHS} 
(for right hand side coupling) and {\sf Matrix} for a matrix coupling algorithm.
Then, for each involved PDE, we have
to specify the input coupling terms, which can be {\sf nodeCoupling},
{\sf surfaceCoupling} and {\sf regionCoupling}. At the end, we have to define
the stopping criteria and the maximum number of iterations
within the section {\sf nonlinear}. Therefore, the general structure 
looks as follows


\begin{verbatim}
<couplingList>
  <iterativePde1Pde2 method="RHS">
     <Pde1>
        <nodeCoupling type=" " name="  "/>
     </Pde1>
     <Pde2>
        <interfaceCoupling type=" " name="  "/>
     </Pde2>
     <nonLinear logging="yes">
       <StopCrit name=" " value="1e-3"/>
       <maxNumIters>  200</maxNumIters>
     </nonLinear>
  </iterativePde1Pde2>

  <iterativePde3Pde4 method="Matrix">
     <Pde3>
        <nodeCoupling type=" " name="  "/>
     </Pde3>
     <Pde4>
        <regionCoupling type=" " name="  "/>
     </Pde4>
     <nonLinear logging="no">
       <StopCrit name=" " value="1e-2"/>
       <maxNumIters> 20 </maxNumIters>
     </nonLinear>
  </iterativePde3Pde4>
</couplingList>
\end{verbatim}

\noindent
For the stopping criteria, we have currently the following names:

\begin{verbatim}
    <StopCrit name="mechdisplacement" value="1e-2"/>
    <StopCrit name="elecForce" value="1e-2"/>
    <StopCrit name="magForce" value="1e-2"/>
\end{verbatim}

\section{ElecMech}
The two involved fields are the electrostatic and the mechanical field.
Often we need a additional PDE for the smoothing of the mesh (moving/deforming 
body problem).

\bigskip
\noindent
{\it Example:}

\begin{verbatim}
<couplingList>
  <iterativeElecMech method="RHS">
     <mechanic>
        <nodeCoupling type="elecForceVWP" name="electrode1"/>
     </mechanic>
     <electrostatic>
        <regionCoupling type="smoothDisplacement" name="air1"/>
        <regionCoupling type="smoothDisplacement" name="air2"/>
     </electrostatic>
     <smooth>
        <nodeCoupling type="mechDisplacement" name="electrode1 "/>
     </smooth>
     <nonLinear logging="no">
       <StopCrit name="mechdisplacement" value="1e-2"/>
       <StopCrit name="elecForce" value="1e-2"/>
       <maxNumIters> 20 </maxNumIters>
     </nonLinear>
  </iterativeAcouMech>
</couplingList>
\end{verbatim}

\bigskip
\noindent

\begin{verbatim}

     </nonLinear>
  </iterativeMagMech>

\section{AcouMech}
Here we differ between an indirect and a direct coupling. Currently we have just 
implemented the iterative coupling.

\bigskip
\noindent
{\it Example:}

\begin{verbatim}
<couplingList>
  <iterativeAcouMech method="RHS">
     <mechanic>
        <nodeCoupling type="acousticForce" name="surf1 "/>
     </mechanic>
     <acoustic>
        <nodeCoupling type="mechForce" name="surf1"/>
     </acoustic>
     <nonLinear logging="no">
       <StopCrit name="mechdisplacement" value="1e-2"/>
       <maxNumIters> 20 </maxNumIters>
     </nonLinear>
  </iterativeAcouMech>
</couplingList>
\end{verbatim}
